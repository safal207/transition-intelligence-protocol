# Example: Startup Decision

## Situation

A founder is considering whether to pivot a product.

## State

The product has early interest, but users do not return consistently.

## Tension

There is a gap between curiosity and habit. People understand the idea, but it is not urgent enough in daily workflow.

## Cause

The current product may be solving a broad problem instead of a painful, narrow one.

## Transition

Move from a general productivity idea to a narrow decision-support protocol for high-stakes human-AI workflows.

## Cooperation check

Participants:

- founder: needs focus and proof;
- early users: need a clearer benefit;
- future partners: need a sharper category;
- contributors: need a stable direction.

The pivot is cooperative if it makes the product easier to understand, test, and support.

## Action

Run a narrow pilot before rewriting the whole project.

## Review question

Did the pilot produce stronger retention, clearer language, or better user trust?
