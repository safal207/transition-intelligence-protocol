# Example: Family Conflict

## Situation

Two family members argue about a practical decision.

## State

Both people want the family to be okay, but each sees a different immediate priority.

## Tension

One person feels pressure to act quickly. The other feels unheard or pushed.

## Cause

The conflict is not only about the surface topic. It may be caused by fatigue, fear, responsibility, or a need for respect.

## Transition

Move from reactive argument to shared problem definition.

## Cooperation check

Participants:

- person A: needs recognition of urgency;
- person B: needs recognition of concern;
- children or family system: need stability and emotional safety.

Cooperation becomes stable when both sides can say what they are protecting.

## Action

Pause the argument and ask:

```text
What are we both trying to protect right now?
```

Then choose the smallest next step that reduces risk without humiliating either side.

## Review question

Did the conversation preserve dignity and make the next state more stable?
